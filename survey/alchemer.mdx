Alchemer is a common choice among research firms and brand-side researchers who need a real gate, not a workaround. The **Login/Password Action** is what makes it work: load your VerifyYou tokens as one-time passwords and Alchemer enforces them before a single question renders. A bad or missing token gets an error screen. The survey never opens.

That is a true gate, enforced by the platform itself — not skip-logic buried inside your question flow.

The screener criterion you are adding is: real, unique, live human. A HumanCheck sits in front of your survey like any other screener criterion. Only respondents who pass land in your sample. The rest never see your questions.

## How the two legs connect

The respondent completes the quick liveness and uniqueness check and lands back on your return URL with `?vyt=TOKEN&vyc=1` appended automatically. That return URL points at your Alchemer survey. A thin entry page or redirect reads `vyt` and pre-fills the Login/Password field. Alchemer checks the token against your pre-loaded list. Valid and unused: the survey opens. Anything else: blocked.

Your survey question flow is untouched. Enforcement lives entirely in the access layer.

<Note>
`vyc=1` on the return URL is a UI convenience hint, not an authoritative result. Always confirm server-side via the token — never gate on the query parameter alone.
</Note>

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a Login/Password Action to your survey">
    In Alchemer, go to **Tools > Action > Login/Password** and place the action before your first content page. Set the mode to **Individual Passwords** (one-time use).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate tokens and load them into Alchemer">
    Generate a batch of one-time tokens for your Flow — at least as many as your expected sample size, with headroom for retries and drop-offs. Export as CSV and upload that list into the Alchemer Login/Password Action.

    For large panels managed programmatically, you can push tokens in via the Alchemer API instead of a manual upload.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Point your VerifyYou success redirect at Alchemer">
    In the VerifyYou dashboard, set the **Success Redirect URL** to your Alchemer survey URL:

    ```
    https://survey.alchemer.com/s3/YOUR_SURVEY_ID
    ```

    VerifyYou appends `?vyt=TOKEN&vyc=1` plus any `pass_params` you have configured (for example, a participant ID from Prolific or MTurk). On the Alchemer side, add a URL variable named `vyt` and map it to pre-fill the Login/Password field. The token arrives pre-filled; the respondent is admitted or rejected without manual entry.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm the OTP burn timing before you go live">
    Before recruiting begins, test when Alchemer marks a one-time password as consumed. See the note below — this is worth verifying in your account before your study fields.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a server-side validate call on submission (optional, recommended)">
    Call `GET /v3/survey/peek?vyt=TOKEN` with a `Bearer pk_val_...` header from a server-side webhook or completion handler the moment a response is submitted. Peek is idempotent — it does not burn the token. If `verified` is false, discard the response. Then call `POST /v3/survey/consume?vyt=TOKEN` once you accept the response as a complete.

    Alchemer's gate is already the primary line of defense. This is belt-and-suspenders — useful if your IRB or client requires a full audit trail confirming each accepted response was independently verified.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
**Confirm when Alchemer burns a one-time password.** Whether the password is marked used on login-click or only on survey completion is worth verifying in your own account before you recruit. If burn happens at completion, a respondent who logs in and then abandons mid-survey could theoretically re-enter on the same token. Test with a dummy password. If burn-on-click is not guaranteed, add the server-side consume call at submission, or set a tight survey timeout so partial sessions cannot be reopened.
</Note>

## Why this beats the URL-variable approach

The older pattern drops `vyt` as a URL variable and uses page-1 skip logic to screen bad values. The structural problem: page 1 has already loaded. The respondent has seen content before they are rejected.

The Login/Password Action runs before any content page renders. A bad or missing token gets an error screen, not a brief glimpse at your screener. That is the difference between a gate and a filter — and it is the same shift researchers make when they replace a trap question with a check that a bot or duplicate cannot pass.

## The validation key

The key you use for server-side peek and consume calls is a per-Flow **publishable** key (`pk_val_...`). It can check and burn tokens for its Flow; it cannot create verifications, read PII, or touch anything outside that Flow. Safe to store in an Alchemer webhook credential or a server-side environment variable.

Do not skip authentication on validation calls. An unauthenticated consume endpoint lets anyone guess token strings and burn them — denial of service — or probe whether a given string is valid. The publishable key prevents both.

Each token carries a TTL of roughly 30 minutes from the moment it is minted. Ask respondents to complete the survey in one session immediately after their check.

## What the uniqueness check actually locks

Alchemer's gate enforces: "this respondent has a valid token." The HumanCheck enforces something stronger: "this face has not passed this Flow before."

A respondent who tries to verify twice gets a second token rejected at minting — before they ever reach the survey door. Repeat-takers, professional survey farmers, and household members attempting the same study are stopped upstream, regardless of how the gate is configured. A collision is caught before a token is ever issued.

The screener criterion you are buying is: real, unique, live human. One complete per face, across the whole Flow.
