Qualtrics is where this integration works hardest. The reason is structural, not cosmetic, and it is worth understanding before you touch a single setting.

## How the gate actually works

A standard Qualtrics survey has an anonymous link anyone can open. We close that link entirely — Invitation Only mode, configured once, no exceptions. Then, for every respondent who passes liveness and uniqueness, we call the Qualtrics Distributions API and generate a **single-use personal distribution link** bound to that respondent and that survey. We redirect them straight to it.

That personal link is both the credential and the gate. It is tied to one verified face, it is spent the moment it is first opened, and a leaked copy dies on the second click. There is no anonymous entry point to probe, no shared URL to forward. By default the link also expires 60 days after the distribution is created — configurable at creation time in Qualtrics, not after. And because the uniqueness check blocks a repeat attempt before the distribution step ever runs, the same face cannot earn a second link no matter how many times they try.

No unverified respondent ever sees your survey questions. That is the promise this flow keeps.

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Turn on Invitation Only in Qualtrics">
    In your Qualtrics survey go to **Survey** > **Survey Protection** and enable **Invitation Only**. This closes the anonymous survey URL. Anyone who navigates to it directly is blocked; only respondents arriving via a personal distribution link can enter.

    Do this first. If you skip it, the gate does not exist regardless of what VerifyYou does downstream.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your Qualtrics API token">
    In the VerifyYou dashboard, open the Flow you are configuring and paste in a scoped Qualtrics API token. The token needs contacts and distributions write permission on the target survey.

    We use this token to call the Distributions API on your behalf: create a contact in a mailing list, create a distribution scoped to both that mailing list and that contact — Qualtrics requires `mailingListId` and `contactId` together on the Create Distribution call, contact ID alone is not enough — then GET the distribution's links and redirect the verified respondent to the personal link. They land in your survey already verified, with no additional token or parameter to handle on your end. The personal link is the proof.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a post-submit Workflow as the backstop">
    In **Workflows**, create a workflow that fires on every new response. Add a condition on the embedded data field `vy_verified`: treat anything other than `true` — including an empty or missing value — as unverified. On that branch, add a **Web Service** task set to `DELETE` against `https://yourdatacenter.qualtrics.com/API/v3/surveys/{surveyId}/responses/{responseId}`, with your `X-API-TOKEN` header set.

    Qualtrics Web Service tasks support `GET`, `POST`, `PUT`, `PATCH`, and `DELETE`, so this entire backstop runs natively inside Qualtrics — no call out to VerifyYou required. If you would rather have VerifyYou execute the delete and keep an audit trail on our side, point the Web Service task at our webhook instead and we will issue the delete for you; that variant is optional, the native path above is simpler and is what we recommend.

    <Warning>
      Qualtrics has a known timing bug: Survey Flow Web Service elements sometimes fire at the **end** of the survey rather than at their position in the flow. This is exactly why a Workflow-level backstop matters more than any in-survey check. If you add a Survey Flow Web Service element to double-check status at entry, treat both `false` **and** an empty or missing `vy_verified` value as unverified — a blank result means the element timed out or fired too late, not that the respondent passed. Never rely on a Survey Flow Web Service element alone.
    </Warning>

    This is the net below the net. Any response that reached submit without a confirmed `vy_verified` value is deleted before it lands in your dataset. You never see it in a CSV, never count it as a complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why two layers

Each one catches something the other misses.

| Layer | What it catches |
|---|---|
| Invitation Only + personal distribution links | Anyone without a VerifyYou-issued link — anonymous access, guessed URLs, forwarded links that have already been used |
| Post-submit Workflow DELETE | Any response that reached submit through an edge case: a timing bug, a Qualtrics platform anomaly, or a verification status that could not be confirmed at the Workflow condition check |

The honest caveat: a response exists briefly in Qualtrics between submit and deletion — the Workflow fires after the fact, not at the door. For virtually all research purposes this is acceptable, and the response is gone before any analysis or export runs. The personal link layer stops the overwhelming majority of bad traffic before they ever touch your questions.

## What each side ends up with

| | Respondent | You |
|---|---|---|
| After verification | Personal distribution link, one use | Confirmed: real, unique, live human |
| At survey entry | Arrives already verified, no extra step | No anonymous traffic; every opener is a confirmed face |
| At completion | Survey submitted normally | Response in dataset with `vy_verified = true` |
| If something slips | Hits the screen-out condition or response is deleted | Response never reaches your analysis |

## How the distribution link works

When a respondent passes verification, we call the Qualtrics Distributions API and mint a link bound to your specific Flow, their verified identity, and your survey. That link:

- Is valid for one use only — it is spent the instant it is first opened, not after a full submit. Qualtrics native single-use distribution links enforce this at the platform level. By default it also expires 60 days after creation; that window is configurable when the distribution is created, not afterward.
- Cannot be earned a second time by the same face — the uniqueness check blocks a repeat attempt before the Distributions API call ever runs.
- Carries no `?vyt=` parameter on your end. The verification already happened; the personal link is the artifact that carries that proof into Qualtrics.

Our systems receive a verdict — real and unique, or not — and a stable respondent identifier. Your survey only ever sees a yes or no. No face, no image, no PII crosses to you.

If you are building a custom integration that also calls our API server-side — for example to confirm status independently — use [the token overview](/the-token) and the validate endpoint for the full peek and consume model.

## Requirements

- A Qualtrics account with API access enabled.
- A scoped API token with contacts and distributions write permission on the target survey.
- **Invitation Only** turned on before you go live.
- Your VerifyYou Flow configured with the Qualtrics integration and the API token connected.

## Questions researchers ask

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can a respondent forward their personal link to a friend?">
    The link is single-use. The moment the first person clicks it, it is gone. A forwarded copy returns an error. And the face that earned the link cannot earn a second one — uniqueness blocks the repeat attempt before we even call Distributions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What about the timing bug — does it affect my data?">
    The timing bug affects Survey Flow Web Service elements, which can fire at the end of a survey rather than at their position. That is why the Workflow-level DELETE is the real backstop, not an in-survey check. The Workflow fires on completion, sees the missing or false `vy_verified` value, and deletes the response. Your dataset only ever contains responses where `vy_verified = true`.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to handle the vyt token or vyc parameter?">
    No. In this integration the personal distribution link is the gate. Verification happens before your survey URL is ever involved. There is no `?vyt=` token for your survey to process.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does VerifyYou store my respondents' faces?">
    No. Your survey receives a verdict and a stable respondent identifier. We hold a face vector — a mathematical representation — used to enforce uniqueness. No image, no face, and no PII is shared with your survey or stored in a way you can access.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens when a respondent fails the check?">
    They return to your recruitment platform's screen-out URL. Your Qualtrics survey never sees them. A failure redirect from VerifyYou takes care of routing; you configure that URL in your Flow settings.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
