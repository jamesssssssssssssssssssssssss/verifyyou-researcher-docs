Every screener has one question it cannot actually enforce: **are they a person, and are they unique?** This guide sets that up on Alchemer, click by click — then shows exactly what your respondent sees, and what happens when someone comes back.

## Exactly how it works

Five moving parts, all of them simple:

1. We generate a batch of **one-time passwords** for your study. Only we can mint them, and each one works exactly once. You load them into Alchemer's own password gate.
2. You share **our study link** instead of your Alchemer link. Everyone who clicks it does the face check first: a live person, then a face we haven't already seen for this study.
3. Each person who passes is handed **the next unused password** and forwarded to your survey, with the password riding along in the link.
4. Your Alchemer login page reads the password from the link and **fills itself in**. Alchemer's own server checks it: valid and unused means the survey opens; anything else means zero questions shown.
5. A spent password is **dead forever**, and the same face can never earn a second one. A shared link, a reused password, or a second attempt all hit a wall — and your export tells you exactly who came through verified.

## Your setup, click by click

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a verification and paste your survey link">
    In the VerifyYou dashboard: click **New verification**, name the study, and pick **Alchemer** under "Where does your survey live?" — that choice is how we know to mint one-time passwords, the credential Alchemer's own gate understands. Paste your Alchemer survey link and click **Create verification**.

    You get back two things: your **study link** (the one you share; your Alchemer link stays private) and your **one-time password list**.

    <Frame caption="Dashboard mockup — a simple screen we are finishing now. What powers it, the hosted check and per-person single-use credentials, is live in the product today.">
      <img src="/images/setup/mock-create.png" alt="VerifyYou new verification screen" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste your passwords into Alchemer">
    Open your survey in the builder. On **page 1**, click **Add New: Action**, pick **Login/Password**, and choose **"Individual (one-time) passwords that deactivate after use."** Paste your password list, one per line. Keep this action alone on page 1 — it gates everything after it.

    <Frame caption="Alchemer's own UI, and Alchemer's own wording: passwords 'deactivate after use.' Their server enforces the gate before a single question shows.">
      <img src="/images/setup/alchemer-login-editor-2.png" alt="Alchemer one-time passwords editor" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the password to fill itself in">
    In the same action, open the **Display** tab and set **Pre-populate Password** to the merge code `[url("vypw")]`. Click **Save Action**. Now the password each verified person carries on their link lands in the box on its own — they type nothing.

    <Frame caption="Alchemer's own UI. The example shown uses a contact merge code; yours uses the URL one above.">
      <img src="/images/setup/alchemer-prepopulate.png" alt="Alchemer pre-populate password setting" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share your study link">
    Paste the study link from step 1 into your panel, your sample provider, or your invite email. That's it — everyone who reaches your survey came through the check.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens when they come back

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Back to the same study: a dead end, on purpose">
    Their face already earned its one password, and that password is spent. A second device, a new browser, a cleared cache: none of it helps, because the check recognizes the **face**, not the machine. Your study stays one person, one response.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Back for your next study: welcome back">
    A returning verified human is recognized and breezes through with less friction, then gets a **fresh one-time password** for the new study — so uniqueness still holds per study. You are never charged twice for the same human. And if you want a full re-scan on every study, that is a setting, and it is your call.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The fifteen-minute proof

No real study needed. Make up a survey, put the gate in front of it, and try to beat it yourself.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set it up">Steps 1 through 4 above, on a made-up survey. About fifteen minutes of clicking, no code.</Step>
  <Step title="Try to beat it">Take the survey through the study link; it just works. Try to take it again, and you can't. Try the raw Alchemer URL with no password, and you get nothing.</Step>
  <Step title="Check the ledger">Export the CSV and see the verified column against your response IDs. Compare verified against unverified on a real dataset — that comparison, run by you and not by us, is worth more than anything we could say.</Step>
</Steps>
