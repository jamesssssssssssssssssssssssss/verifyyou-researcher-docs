The recruitment leg changes exactly two things: where you paste the gated link, and what a rejection costs. Everything else stays the same.

Respondents click the link, pass liveness and uniqueness, and land on your survey with `?vyt=TOKEN` appended. Your recruitment platform never touches our API. The variation across platforms is where that link physically lives and what happens — financially and logistically — when someone fails the check.

<Info>
The recruitment leg and the survey leg are independent choices. This page covers recruitment only. For enforcement inside the survey, see [Survey platforms overview](/survey/overview).
</Info>

## What a screen-out actually costs you

A weak gate is not just a data-quality problem. On most panels, a bad complete costs you more than a screen-out would — but the math shifts by platform.

On **Prolific**, a screen-out is a paid event. Roughly $0.19 per rejection, regardless of whether the respondent touched your survey. A respondent who clears your HumanCheck but slips past a soft survey gate becomes a full complete you pay for whether the data is clean or not. Stronger enforcement at the survey leg saves money, not just cleaner data.

On **MTurk**, a worker who cannot complete the HIT earns nothing. The screen-out is free. That changes the calculus: a true gate via the Qualification mechanism is both the cheapest and the cleanest option.

On **Cint and Lucid**, terminated respondents are free in most panel agreements. The risk is not the fee — it is a missing S2S postback that leaves the panel marking the session as a drop-off instead of a clean terminate, which degrades your standing with the provider over time.

On **your own list**, there is no panel fee at all. A "not eligible" page is all you need.

## Platform comparison

| Platform | Where the link goes | Screen-out cost | Best hardening |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prolific | External study URL field | Screen-out fee (~$0.19 per reject, paid) | Secure External URL with signed JWT verified at entry |
| MTurk | HIT external URL | Free — worker earns nothing | Custom Qualification: workers without it cannot see the HIT |
| CloudResearch | Project link | Small screen-out payment required | Set partial-completes policy to unpaid where the platform permits |
| Cint / Lucid | Panel entry link (carries `RID`) | Free terminate in most agreements | S2S postback so the panel records a clean close, not a drop-off |
| Your own list | Paste the link directly | None — you control the return page | A "not eligible" landing that checks `vyc=0` on load |

Where hardening pays off most: Prolific and MTurk. Prolific because screen-outs cost you real money. MTurk because the Qualification mechanism gives you a true gate — no token, no work, no partial pay — and it is the cheapest option on the platform.

## Using your own list

If you are recruiting from your own email list, a social post, or a direct share, you control the whole flow. Paste the VerifyYou share link directly in the message. No panel fee, no partial-pay exposure.

The one thing to configure is what happens after a failed check. Because a dedicated failure redirect URL does not exist yet (it is on the build list), a respondent who fails lands on your origin URL with `vyc=0`. Build a short "not eligible" page at that origin and check `vyc` on load to show the right message.

<Note>
A failure redirect URL is on the VerifyYou build list. For now, handle `vyc=0` on your return page.
</Note>

## The link itself

However you recruit, the link you share is always the same shape. The no-code option is the share link from your dashboard:

```
https://app.verifyyou.com/verification/FLOW_ID
```

If you need to inject participant IDs at link-generation time — so the panel's respondent identifier rides through the check and comes back on the return URL — call `POST /v3/initialize` and use the returned `url`. The `pass_params` field carries any participant identifier back on the return URL automatically.

```json
{
  "verification_id": "vfy_xxx",
  "external_tracker": "study_autumn_2026",
  "pass_params": { "PROLIFIC_PID": "{{%PROLIFIC_PID%}}" },
  "origin": "https://your-survey-host.com",
  "return_path": "/study/entry"
}
```

`external_tracker` is echoed on webhooks only, not on the return URL. Use it to tie webhook events back to your internal records. `pass_params` is what comes back on the return URL alongside `?vyt=TOKEN`.
