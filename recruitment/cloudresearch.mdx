CloudResearch Connect routes its own panel traffic into your study. The HumanCheck sits between them: participants click your Connect project link, do the quick face scan, a few second liveness selfie, and arrive in your survey only if they pass. The panel never touches your survey directly. The check is the bridge.

## How the two legs fit together

The **recruitment leg** is CloudResearch Connect. It controls the project link, the compensation rules, and the Completion Branches ("Partial Completes") handling.

The **survey leg** is whatever tool you have attached to the project. It holds the token gate and records the response.

The two legs do not talk to each other. They only meet at the VerifyYou gated link in the middle. When a participant passes, VerifyYou carries them — and their participant ID — into the survey. When they fail, you route them back to CloudResearch with the partial-complete URL so the platform registers the event correctly.

## Prerequisites

- A VerifyYou account with a Flow created for this study.
- A CloudResearch Connect project with Completion Branches ("Partial Completes") enabled (required — see Step 2).
- Your survey tool configured with a working survey link.

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get the gated link for your Flow">
    In the VerifyYou dashboard, open your Flow and copy the no-code share link:

    ```
    app.verifyyou.com/verification/FLOW_ID
    ```

    This is what you paste into Connect as the survey URL. Do not paste your survey tool link there directly — the point is that participants hit the HumanCheck first.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable Completion Branches in your Connect project">
    In the Connect project settings, turn on **Completion Branches** (CloudResearch's informal name for this is "Partial Completes"). This lets you return participants who fail verification to CloudResearch with a partial-complete URL instead of leaving them without a path out.

    Once enabled, CloudResearch shows you a partial-complete redirect URL for the project. Copy it. You will need it in the next step.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Handle failed verifications">
    A dedicated failure redirect field in the VerifyYou dashboard is on the build list but is not yet live. Until it ships, handle failures like this:

    - Set your Flow's return page to show a clear end-of-study message for participants who do not pass.
    - Coordinate with CloudResearch support to report failed-verification screen-outs, or instruct participants in your study description to use the provided exit link.

    <Note>
      Check your dashboard before launch. When the failure redirect field becomes available, you can paste the CloudResearch partial-complete URL directly into it, and participants who fail liveness or uniqueness will be forwarded there automatically.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pass the participant ID through the flow">
    VerifyYou echoes whatever you put in `pass_params` back onto the success return URL, so your survey receives the participant identifier without any extra wiring.

    Append `participantId` to the gated link you paste into Connect:

    ```
    app.verifyyou.com/verification/FLOW_ID?participantId={{participantId}}
    ```

    CloudResearch injects the real value at click time via its `{{participantId}}` template variable. VerifyYou then echoes it as a query parameter on the return URL so your survey tool can record it alongside the response.

    Connect also offers `{{assignmentId}}` and `{{projectId}}` as optional template variables. Append them the same way if you want them on the return URL too:

    ```
    app.verifyyou.com/verification/FLOW_ID?participantId={{participantId}}&assignmentId={{assignmentId}}&projectId={{projectId}}
    ```

    <Warning>
      `participantId` is case-sensitive. The casing above is exact. A different casing means the parameter does not appear on the return URL.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Paste the final gated link into your Connect project">
    In the Connect project, set the **Survey URL** to the link you built in Steps 1 and 4:

    ```
    app.verifyyou.com/verification/FLOW_ID?participantId={{participantId}}
    ```

    CloudResearch sends participants there. They do the selfie. They land in your survey — or they do not.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Gate the survey entry point">
    When a participant passes, VerifyYou returns them to your survey with `?vyt=TOKEN&vyc=1` appended to your Flow's configured return URL.

    <Warning>
      `vyc=1` is a UI hint only — it tells your return page that verification passed, but it is not a trusted confirmation. Do not gate entry on `vyc` alone. Always confirm server-side.
    </Warning>

    At entry, call:

    ```
    GET /v3/survey/peek?vyt=TOKEN
    ```

    with an `Authorization: Bearer pk_val_FLOW_RAND` header. This call is idempotent — it does not burn the token. A `verified: true` response means the token is real, unused, tied to this Flow, and not yet expired. Anyone who does not clear that check gets screened out here.

    When the response is recorded as a real complete, burn the token so it cannot be reused:

    ```
    POST /v3/survey/consume?vyt=TOKEN
    ```

    For tool-specific setup, see [Qualtrics](/survey/qualtrics), [Alchemer](/survey/alchemer), or the relevant platform page under [Survey platforms](/survey/overview).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pay policy: what to tell CloudResearch about the screen-out

CloudResearch's pay policy turns on why the participant was screened out:

| Screen-out type | Compensation required |
|---|---|
| Ineligibility — participant does not qualify for your study (including demographics) | At least a prorated partial payment, meeting CloudResearch's pay floor: $7.50/hour minimum, $10/hour recommended, prorated for time spent |
| Failed attention or quality check | $0 permissible — but only via a researcher-configured completion branch, not automatic |

A failed HumanCheck is not automatically a $0 event. $0 is only permitted for participants routed out through a completion branch you configure for failing an attention or quality check — it is not the default outcome of any screen-out. The practical move: position the HumanCheck as a data-quality check in your completion branch setup, and confirm the $0 path with CloudResearch before relying on it.

<Warning>
  Confirm this against CloudResearch's current policy before you go live. What is permissible for attention checks is not guaranteed to extend to third-party verification gates. If in doubt, budget for partial pay on every failure and avoid the ambiguity entirely.
</Warning>

## Trade-offs

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="A true gate on every entry" icon="shield-check">
    Every participant does the liveness selfie before they see a single question. There is no bypass, no post-hoc cleaning, no hoping the trap question caught the bots.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Uniqueness that follows the face, not the account" icon="fingerprint">
    The same person cannot earn a second token. Repeat takers are stopped even if they use a different CloudResearch account or device, because the uniqueness check is face-based, not credential-based.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Failure redirect needs a workaround for now" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    The dashboard field that would automatically forward failed participants to the CloudResearch partial-complete URL is not yet live. Failed verifications require a message page and manual coordination until it ships.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Pay policy ambiguity" icon="circle-question">
    $0 for a failed verification is only permitted through a researcher-configured completion branch for a quality check, not automatic, and it turns on CloudResearch's current interpretation of that policy. Confirm before launch, or default to prorated partial pay and remove the ambiguity.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## The token in plain terms

When a participant passes the selfie, VerifyYou mints a random opaque string and records it as: real, unused, tied to this Flow, expiring in about 30 minutes. That string rides to your survey as `?vyt=TOKEN` in the URL. When your survey hands it back via a `peek` call, we look it up: is it real? Unused? For this Flow? Not expired? Only "yes to all four" passes — and `peek` does not consume it. The `consume` call burns it once, after you have decided the response counts. It cannot be guessed. It cannot be earned twice by the same face. A repeat taker never gets a valid token to hand in — which is the point.
