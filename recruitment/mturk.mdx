MTurk is an open marketplace. That is its strength and its problem. Anyone with a worker account can accept your HIT, which means the population accepting it is, by default, unfiltered. Bots are real. Repeat-takers are real. A professional survey-farmer who has completed variants of your study before is real. The Qualification gate exists precisely for this.

The advantage MTurk has over most platforms: the strongest enforcement option is also the cheapest. A custom Qualification blocks unverified workers from accepting your main HIT, and with the right setting, from finding or previewing it at all. A worker who fails the HumanCheck and returns the assignment without submitting costs you nothing. That protection is not automatic, though: if a worker submits the verification HIT instead of returning it, MTurk auto-approves and charges you after your account's approval delay elapses, whether or not you take any action.

## How the Qualification flow works

The recruitment leg starts on MTurk. A short, low-cost verification HIT sits in the middle. Your main survey HIT is gated behind a custom Qualification that only workers who pass can hold.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the VerifyYou Verified qualification">
    VerifyYou creates a custom Qualification in your requester account via the MTurk Requester API — named something like "VerifyYou Verified - Study XYZ". It is non-assignable by workers. Only your account can grant it. You get back a `QualificationTypeId`; keep it, you will need it for the main HIT.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initialize a session and post a short verification HIT">
    Call `/v3/initialize` from your server to create a verification session. MTurk appends `workerId`, `assignmentId`, `hitId`, and `turkSubmitTo` automatically when a worker opens an external URL HIT — include them in `pass_params` so they return to your completion page alongside the token.

    ```json
    POST /v3/initialize
    {
      "verification_id": "your-verification-id",
      "external_tracker": "mturk-qual-batch-01",
      "pass_params": { "workerId": "WORKER_ID", "assignmentId": "ASSIGNMENT_ID", "hitId": "HIT_ID" },
      "origin": "https://your-completion-page.example.com",
      "return_path": "/mturk/complete"
    }
    ```

    The response is `{ url }` — a session URL at `app.verifyyou.com/v/SESSION`. Use that URL as the external URL in your verification HIT.

    A note on `external_tracker`: it is echoed on webhooks only, not on the return URL. Use `pass_params` to carry anything you need back at completion.

    Set the HIT reward low — typically $0.05 to $0.10. That is enough to attract workers without paying your real incentive for a check you want to keep cheap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="On pass: VerifyYou submits the HIT and grants the Qualification">
    When a worker passes the liveness and uniqueness check, the return page (still inside the HIT, in the worker's own browser) auto-submits the assignment by POSTing the `assignmentId` to the `turkSubmitTo` URL MTurk supplied. MTurk requires that POST to come from the worker's browser session; a server cannot submit it out-of-band. VerifyYou then grants your Qualification to that `workerId` server-side via the `AssociateQualificationWithWorker` API operation (the older `AssignQualification` name you may see in some references is deprecated).

    The worker returns to `origin` + `return_path` with `?vyt=TOKEN&vyc=1` plus any `pass_params` you specified. Confirm server-side — `vyc` is a UI hint only, not a guarantee.

    To verify the token, call peek from your completion page:

    ```
    GET /v3/survey/peek?vyt=TOKEN
    Authorization: Bearer pk_val_FLOW_RAND
    ```

    Peek is idempotent and does not burn the token. It returns `{ verified, reason }`. Workers who return the HIT unsubmitted cost you nothing. If a failed worker submits the assignment anyway rather than returning it, MTurk will auto-approve and charge you once your requester-set approval delay elapses (up to 30 days) unless you actively reject it first — silence is not free on a submitted assignment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post your main HIT with the Qualification requirement">
    Create your real survey or task HIT with a `QualificationRequirement` that the worker must hold your VerifyYou Verified qualification. This is the step that matters most: by default, MTurk's `ActionsGuarded` value is `"Accept"`, which only blocks unqualified workers from accepting the HIT — they can still find it in search and open the preview. To make the gate invisible, you must explicitly set `ActionsGuarded` to `"DiscoverPreviewAndAccept"`:

    ```json
    {
      "QualificationTypeId": "your-qual-type-id",
      "Comparator": "EqualTo",
      "IntegerValues": [1],
      "ActionsGuarded": "DiscoverPreviewAndAccept"
    }
    ```

    With `ActionsGuarded: DiscoverPreviewAndAccept` set, non-qualified workers cannot find, preview, or accept the HIT at all. Leave it at the MTurk default and they can still see and preview your main HIT, they just cannot click Accept.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The Qualification is a true gate, not a soft one, as long as you set `ActionsGuarded` to `DiscoverPreviewAndAccept` on the main HIT. A worker with no verified token never reaches your survey questions. There is no after-the-fact deletion step, no CSV cleanup, no post-hoc dedup.
</Note>

## Simpler mode: paste the gated link directly

If you skip the Qualification setup, post a single HIT whose external URL is the VerifyYou session URL from `/v3/initialize`. Workers who pass are redirected to your survey. Instruct workers who fail to return the HIT unsubmitted — a returned assignment costs you nothing. Reserve rejection for clear bad-faith submissions (a worker who fails and submits anyway with fabricated survey answers), not as a routine screen-out tool: mass rejection dings worker approval ratings and your reputation as a requester, and the community norm here is exclusion, not punishment.

Simpler, but weaker for one reason: your main survey link is visible to anyone who accepts the HIT. A worker who fails the check could navigate to it directly if your survey has no independent entry enforcement. Pair this mode with a token check at the survey start — see [Qualtrics](/survey/qualtrics) for one approach, or call `POST /v3/survey/consume?vyt=TOKEN` on submission to burn the token and block any second pass.

## Trade-offs

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Qualification flow" icon="shield-check">
    True gate, with `ActionsGuarded: DiscoverPreviewAndAccept` set. Non-verified workers never see your main HIT. Workers who fail and return the verification HIT unsubmitted cost you nothing. VerifyYou grants the Qualification automatically on pass. Best when panel purity is the priority.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Direct link HIT" icon="link">
    No Qualification setup required. Initialize a session, paste the returned URL, post the HIT. Instruct workers who fail to return it unsubmitted, at no cost to you. Weaker entry control unless your destination survey independently enforces the token. Good for quick pilots or when you add a consume call on the survey side.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Why uniqueness is what actually protects your data

A qualification code can be shared. A face cannot.

The same person cannot earn a second VerifyYou token from the same study. The uniqueness check recognises them and declines — a collision. That stops the repeat-taker and the professional survey-farmer at the source, before they touch your data, rather than catching duplicates in a post-hoc pass you may or may not remember to run.

One honest limit: the token TTL is roughly 30 minutes. A worker who earns the Qualification and then accepts the main HIT long after verifying will find the token in their return URL expired. The Qualification itself does not expire, so the gate still holds; you just cannot peek the token server-side after that window. For most MTurk workflows the gap between earning the Qualification and starting the main HIT is well inside 30 minutes.
