Trust breaks at scale.

The moment a survey is worth money, people find a way. Bots fill out completes. One person quietly runs fifteen identities to farm the incentive. Bulk respondents churn through your screener. And the more you pay per respondent, the more it is worth someone's while to be a respondent they should not be.

You feel it as three quiet costs. Fake and duplicate respondents wreck data quality, so the story you tell your client is built on sample that was never real. They inflate what you actually pay: every bad complete is money spent on a row you have to throw away. And they drive drop-off and re-fielding, because a panel you cannot trust is a panel you keep re-running.

Here is the part nobody says out loud. Everyone buying sample already wonders, privately, whether it is real. It is the unspoken thing behind every delivery. You hope the trap question caught the worst of it, you smooth over the rest, and you deliver. The doubt does not go away, it just goes unspoken.

## The trust layer between nothing and KYC

VerifyYou is that layer, applied to research. A HumanCheck confirms one thing before a respondent counts: a real, unique, live human. Live, so it is a person present now, not a photo, a replay, a mask, or a generated face. Unique, so the same person cannot quietly become fifteen respondents.

It does this without the cost, the PII, or the friction of an ID check. Your survey never sees a face, a name, or an image. It sees a verdict: real and unique, yes or no. We take on the face; you only ever get the answer.

It is not KYC, and that is the point. KYC proves a legal identity, and it costs real money, collects real PII, and turns away real respondents at the door. For a survey you do not need to know who someone is. You need to know they are a real, unique human, and only that. The lightest check that still protects what is at stake, so your real respondents barely notice.

## What each check actually gets you

Most surveys sit at one of these four points. They are not equivalent, and the two in the middle are where the confusion lives.

| | No checks | Attention / trap question | HumanCheck | Full KYC |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| What it proves | Nothing | They read one question | A real, unique, live human | A specific legal identity |
| Stops duplicate respondents | No | No | Yes | Yes |
| Resists bots and AI faces | No | Weakly (a bot can pass a written trap) | Yes | Yes |
| PII held | None | None | None (face vector, never an image) | Full identity documents |
| Friction for a real respondent | None | Low, but annoying | Low, a quick liveness check | High, an ID upload |
| Cost per complete | Hidden (bad data) | Hidden (still lets bots through) | About two cents | Dollars, plus lost respondents |

An attention or trap question is the tool most teams already reach for. It is a fake question you hope catches a bad respondent. A HumanCheck is the modern replacement: instead of a question a bot can read and pass, a real check a bot or a duplicate cannot pass. It does not catch inattention, it catches unreality, which is the thing the trap question was always a proxy for.

## The core promise, made concrete

One verified human per response keeps the panel clean. Not "fewer bad completes on average" — one real, unique person behind each row you pay for. A duplicate is caught the second time the same face appears. A generated face never clears liveness. What lands in your data is what you meant to buy.

And it changes what you can say. Instead of hoping the trap question held, you can tell your client something plain and true: I verify every respondent is a real, unique human. The doubt that was always in the room is gone, and you are the one who removed it.

The cost of that is a rounding error on sample spend. About two cents on a three-to-seven-dollar complete. Forty dollars on a ten-thousand-dollar sample. No one calls that a premium — compare it to the twelve to fifteen dollars you already waste on a single bad complete.

## Questions teams ask

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Will it hurt completion?">
    A check turns some traffic away, and most of what it turns away is the traffic you did not want. A real respondent does a quick liveness check once and continues; on the return moment, quiet device recognition can make it near-invisible. What drops off is bots, duplicates, and incentive-farmers — the completes you were paying for and throwing away.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is this KYC?">
    No. KYC proves a legal identity and collects identity documents. A HumanCheck proves only that a respondent is a real, unique, live human, and holds no identity data. It is the lighter check that sits between no checks and full KYC — enough to protect your sample, not so much that it becomes an ID gate.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What do you store, and what does my survey see?">
    Your survey sees a verdict only — real and unique, yes or no — and a stable id, never a face, a name, or an image. Uniqueness works from a privacy-preserving face vector, a mathematical representation, not a stored photo. The PII stops with us; none of it crosses to you.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
