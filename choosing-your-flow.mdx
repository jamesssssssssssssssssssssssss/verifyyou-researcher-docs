Every VerifyYou integration is the same shape. Once you see it, the question stops being "which integration do I use" and becomes two independent choices: where your respondents come from, and where they land after the check.

The check sits right at the point of value, the moment before someone becomes a paid complete. It is one of your screener criteria: is this a real person, and are they the only one? It answers that before the respondent counts.

## The shape, every time

<Steps>
  <Step title="Respondents click your gated link">
    Instead of the direct survey link, you send or embed a VerifyYou link. That is either the no-code share link from your dashboard or a `POST /v3/initialize` URL your system mints. The link records where to send the respondent when the check finishes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The HumanCheck runs">
    The respondent completes a short hosted flow at `app.verifyyou.com/v/SESSION`. We check liveness first — a live human present now, not a photo, replay, mask, or generated face — then uniqueness: have we seen this exact face before in your study? One human, one token.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Forward, or screen out">
    On a pass we redirect back to your survey with `?vyt=TOKEN&vyc=1` appended. Any participant id you passed in — a Prolific PID, for instance — rides along on the return URL through `pass_params`. On a fail the respondent returns with `vyc=0` and your return page routes the screen-out. A dedicated failure redirect URL is on the build list; it does not exist yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The token is enforced">
    Your survey platform, a webhook, or your own code checks the token. How tightly you check it is exactly what you are choosing when you pick a survey leg.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The two legs never talk to each other. They only touch our gated link in the middle. That is why any recruitment leg works with any survey leg: you are not wiring them together, you are each connecting to us.
</Note>

## The two choices

### Recruitment leg: where respondents start

The recruitment leg sets the cost of a rejection. If someone fails on Prolific, Prolific counts it as a screen-out and they earn nothing or a small flat fee. If you cold-emailed them, a rejection costs you almost nothing. The leg does not change the check; it changes what a failing respondent costs you upstream.

| Option | Rejection cost | How you use the gated link |
|---|---|---|
| Prolific | Screen-out, respondent earns nothing | Paste as the study URL; add `?PROLIFIC_PID={{%PROLIFIC_PID%}}` to carry the id through. Prolific's Secure External URL signs entry in a short-lived JWT we verify at entry. |
| MTurk | HIT is not completable | Use as the external URL in your HIT; a custom Qualification is both a true gate and the cheapest option. |
| Panel / sample company | Panel removes the respondent | Send the link as your entry URL. Cint uses an S2S postback — browser redirects fail inside panel webviews. |
| Your own list | Low — you emailed them | Share the link directly or embed it. |
| Organic / social | Very low | Post or embed the link. |

See [Recruitment options](/recruitment/overview) for the per-platform setup.

### Survey leg: where respondents land

The survey leg sets the strength of the lock. This is really a question of when a bad respondent is caught, not whether.

| Tier | When caught | How |
|---|---|---|
| True gate | Before any question loads | Single-use personal links, a login/password gate, an MTurk qualification, or a wrapper page you host that calls `GET /v3/survey/peek` before rendering — backed by a webhook that calls `POST /v3/survey/consume` to burn the token on a real completion. |
| Soft gate + webhook | The instant they submit | A platform webhook calls `GET /v3/survey/peek` to validate; bad responses are auto-deleted through the platform API. `POST /v3/survey/consume` burns the token on a real completion so it cannot be reused. |
| Direct call | Before your own server saves | Your code calls `GET /v3/survey/peek`, then `POST /v3/survey/consume` on a real completion. |

<Note>
`GET /v3/survey/peek` is idempotent — it reads the token's status without burning it. `POST /v3/survey/consume` burns the token exactly once. In every tier, consume fires on the real completion; peek is the pre-check that decides whether to let the respondent through.
</Note>

See [Survey platform guides](/survey/overview) for the per-platform setup.

## Three flows worth copying

### Prolific + Qualtrics, the gold standard

The tightest setup there is. Nobody unverified ever loads a question.

- Set your Prolific study URL to the VerifyYou gated link with `?PROLIFIC_PID={{%PROLIFIC_PID%}}` appended.
- In the dashboard, point the return path at your Qualtrics survey. The recommended path is **single-use personal distribution links**: import them into the Qualtrics Contacts panel and each link is consumed on first load. A respondent who fails the check never receives one, so there is no URL for them to visit.
- Back the gate with a post-submit Workflow that DELETEs a bad response through the Qualtrics REST API.

You end up with a true gate. The link is bound to a single verified respondent — a shared or stolen link does not help because the human who earned it is already recorded.

<Warning>
If you use Qualtrics Web Service to validate the token at the top of your Survey Flow instead of single-use links, there is a known end-of-survey timing bug: the Web Service call can return empty. Treat an empty `verified` field as unverified, not verified. Single-use personal links avoid this window entirely and are the recommended path. The Web Service path should be backed by a post-submit Workflow that DELETEs bad responses through the REST API.
</Warning>

### Prolific + Tally, the common case

Tally has no single-use entry credentials, so you reach a true gate a different way and add a webhook backstop.

- Set the Prolific study URL to your VerifyYou gated link.
- In the dashboard, point the return path at a small wrapper page you host — a few lines of JS or a Vercel edge function. The wrapper calls `GET /v3/survey/peek?vyt=TOKEN` before it redirects to the Tally form. Anyone without a valid token sees an error page, never the form.
- As a backstop, add a Tally webhook. On every submission the webhook calls `GET /v3/survey/peek` to re-validate, then `POST /v3/survey/consume` to burn the token. Invalid responses are DELETEd through the Tally API — the key upgrade over flagging — so they never reach your export.

You end up with a true gate at the wrapper and an auto-delete backstop behind it. The wrapper blocks unverified entry; the webhook catches any edge case and burns the token on a real completion so it cannot be reused.

### Your own list + Formspark, the lightest

You are emailing a small list. Lower-risk than a crowdwork panel, and you own the form.

- Email respondents the no-code share link from the dashboard. Set the success Redirect URL to your Formspark form.
- Formspark is a direct-call flow, already optimal. In your JS submit handler, read the `vyt` query parameter we appended to the return URL and, before the native submit saves anything, call `GET /v3/survey/peek?vyt=TOKEN` with your `pk_val_FLOW_RAND` key in the `Authorization` header. If `verified` is false, reject.
- On a real completion, call `POST /v3/survey/consume` once to burn the token.

You end up with direct-call enforcement, gated before save. Because `peek` is idempotent and does not burn the token, you can call it earlier in the flow — from a progress-save handler, for instance — without spending it. `consume` fires exactly once, when a real completion is accepted.

## Mix and match

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Any recruitment leg" icon="shuffle">
    Prolific, MTurk, a sample company, your own list, organic traffic. The check is identical either way. You only change where the gated link lives and what a rejection costs the respondent.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Any survey leg" icon="lock">
    Qualtrics, Tally, Typeform, Jotform, SurveyMonkey, Google Forms, Formspark, your own app. The enforcement tier is set by what the platform supports, not by anything on our side.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Because the two legs only ever meet at our gated link, swapping one end does not touch the other. A Prolific study can move from Qualtrics to Tally without changing the Prolific study URL. A Tally form can switch from an organic share link to Prolific without touching the Tally webhook. They stack, they do not substitute.

<Tip>
Choose the survey leg first. That tells you the enforcement tier you will get. Then pick the recruitment leg that matches the rejection cost you want. The pair you land on is your flow.
</Tip>

## The honest catch

The token is a claim ticket, not a cryptographic proof welded to the survey session. We mint one, and only one, per unique human, then look it up when someone presents it. What we cannot control is the window between "check passed" and "token burned by your survey." In a true-gate setup that window is zero: the token is burned before a single question loads. In a webhook setup the respondent does see the form and completes it, but the bad response is deleted before it lands in your export. For most research, the two outcomes are the same where it counts: no bad data reaches your analysis.

Uniqueness is what makes the token mean more than "a live human clicked." The same person cannot earn a second token for the same study, so even the softest enforcement tier stops the repeat-taker — the one bad respondent researchers care about most.
