The moment something is worth gaming, a survey complete, a signup, a payout, a gated offer, people find a way: bots, fake accounts, one person quietly running fifteen identities, bulk buyers. Trust breaks at that scale. VerifyYou is the layer between nothing and KYC, and this is how you put it to work.

You drop a **HumanCheck** in front of the action you care about, on the platform you already use. Your survey tool, checkout page, or signup form never has to know we exist. You deploy the check; the platform just sees a respondent who either passed or did not. It is a real, unique, live human, without the cost, the PII, or the friction of an ID check. It is not KYC, and that is the point.

<Note>
  These docs lead with academic and market researchers running paid studies, because that is where the doubt bites hardest and the math is cleanest. The same primitive works for any gated action, checkout or signup included. If that is you, read straight on.
</Note>

## One primitive: a claim ticket for one unique face

Every check produces the same thing: a **single-use token bound to one unique face**. Think of it as a claim ticket, a coat-check stub. We mint one the moment a respondent clears **liveness** (a live person present now, not a photo, replay, mask, or generated face) and **uniqueness** (are they new, or has this face been seen before?). We record it on our side as real, unused, for this flow, and give it a short expiry window.

The respondent carries the stub back to your survey. When your survey, a webhook, or a wrapper page hands it back to us, we look it up: real? unused? for this flow? not expired? Only yes to all counts. It cannot be forged, because we are the only one who mints it. It cannot be earned twice, because uniqueness means the same face gets exactly one ticket, so sharing the link or farming completes goes nowhere.

That last part is the lock. A code that only proves a live human clicked can be shared or farmed. A ticket bound to one unique face cannot. Liveness stops bots; uniqueness stops the repeat taker, the panel member completing on two devices, the friend the link got passed to. Those are the problems that actually cost you.

## The two legs

Every integration has the same shape, with two ends you choose independently. They never touch each other, only our gated link in the middle, so there is no matrix of platform pairings to learn.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Recruitment leg">
    Where respondents start. You paste our gated link here, whether that is a Prolific study URL, an MTurk HIT, a Qualtrics mailing list, a panel provider, or a plain social post. This leg decides what a rejection costs the respondent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The HumanCheck">
    A short hosted flow in the browser. We run liveness, then uniqueness. On a pass, we return the respondent to your survey with the token on the URL. On a fail, they come back marked not verified, and your return page routes the screen-out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Survey leg">
    Where the survey or action lives. This leg decides how hard the token is enforced, from a true entry gate where no bad respondent ever sees a question, to an auto-reject webhook that deletes a bad response the instant it is submitted. Either way, a bad token never becomes a paid, counted complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You pick your recruitment source and your survey tool separately, and the same check sits between them. The lightest check that still protects what is at stake, so your real respondents barely notice.
