The whole system rests on one idea: **every password is tied to one unique human and works exactly once.** Everything else is plumbing. This page is the plumbing, explained honestly.

## One password, one human, one use

Think of each password as a claim ticket. We mint it, we record it, and we only hand it to a person who just proved two things on camera: they are **live** (a real person present right now, not a photo, a replay, or a generated face) and they are **unique** (a face we have not already seen for this study).

Your survey does not have to trust us on any of that. It enforces the ticket with Alchemer's own <a href="https://help.alchemer.com/help/login-password-action">Login/Password action</a>, set to **individual one-time passwords that deactivate after use**. That is Alchemer's feature and Alchemer's wording. Their server checks the password before page one, and a used password is refused, full stop.

So the gate holds against every cheap trick:

- **A shared link** carries a password that was already spent the first time it was used. Dead on arrival.
- **A guessed password** does not exist. Only we mint them, and they are random and single-use.
- **A second attempt** fails upstream, before Alchemer: the same face cannot earn a second password for the same study, no matter the device, browser, or phone number.
- **A bot** never gets a password at all, because a replay or generated face fails the liveness scan.

The honest limit: a real, unique human who rushes your survey is still a real, unique human. The check proves who they are, not how carefully they answer, so keep your speeder and attention checks. It replaces the trap question's fraud-catching job, not its attention-catching job.

## What it costs

About one to two cents a verification, charged once per human, so returning respondents never count again. On a $10,000 sample of $3 to $7 completes, the whole check is a rounding error. Compare that to what a single bad complete costs you: the incentive, the sample fee, and the analyst time spent wondering whether to trust the row.

## What we never touch

No photo is shared with you or with Alchemer, only a yes or no. Your survey receives a verdict and a password, never a face, a name, or personal information. We act as the broker in the middle: respondents share the scan with us, and all your study ever learns is "this is a real, unique human."

## Two things we confirm live

We would rather flag these than have you find them.

- **Exactly when Alchemer marks a one-time password as used.** Their docs do not say whether it burns at login or at completion. A five-minute test in the builder settles it, and it is part of any first setup we do together.
- **Whether the pre-filled login can skip itself entirely.** The pieces are documented; the combination is not. Worst case, your respondent sees a filled box and taps one button.
