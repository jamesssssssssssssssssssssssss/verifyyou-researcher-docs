## When someone gets blocked

Two ways to fail the check. Both end the same way: no password, no survey, no complete, nothing to pay.

<div className="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-5 mt-6">
  <div>
    <img className="vy-phone" src="/images/screen-denied.png" alt="Denied screen" style={{ maxWidth: '300px', margin: '0 auto', display: 'block' }} />
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-gray-500 dark:text-zinc-400 text-center"><b className="text-gray-900 dark:text-zinc-50">Not unique.</b> A face we have already seen for this study. Your repeat taker. <i>(Real product.)</i></p>
  </div>
  <div>
    <img className="vy-phone" src="/images/screen-liveness.png" alt="Liveness failure screen" style={{ maxWidth: '300px', margin: '0 auto', display: 'block' }} />
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-gray-500 dark:text-zinc-400 text-center"><b className="text-gray-900 dark:text-zinc-50">Not live.</b> A replay, a photo, or a generated face. Real people in bad lighting get a retry; bots do not. <i>(Real product.)</i></p>
  </div>
</div>

A blocked respondent is routed back where they came from, your panel's screen-out if you set one. They never get a password, so Alchemer's gate never opens. And because the check recognizes the **face**, not the device, a new browser or a second phone changes nothing.
