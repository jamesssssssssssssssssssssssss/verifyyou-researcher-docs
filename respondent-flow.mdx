There are only three things that can happen when someone clicks your study link. They pass and take your survey, they get blocked and never see it, or they come back later. Here is each one, on real product screens, and what it means for you.

## When they pass

The normal case. Click, scan, done. A few seconds, then they are answering your questions.

**Why this matters to you:** the friction lands only on the first-time check, a few seconds, and verified respondents flow into your survey without typing anything. Completion stays high while the gate stays closed.

## When someone gets blocked

Two ways to fail the check, and both end the same way: no password, no survey, no complete.

  </div>
  <div>
    <img className="vy-phone" src="/images/screen-liveness.png" alt="Liveness failure screen" style={{ maxWidth: '340px', margin: '0 auto', display: 'block' }} />
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-gray-500 dark:text-zinc-400 text-center"><b className="text-gray-900 dark:text-zinc-50">Not live.</b> A replay, a photo, or a generated face. Real people in bad lighting get a retry; bots do not get lucky. <i>(Real product.)</i></p>
  </div>
</div>

What happens next for a blocked respondent: they are routed back where they came from, your panel's screen-out if you set one, and that is the end of it. They never receive a password, so Alchemer's gate never opens, so they never see question one.

**Why this matters to you:** a blocked respondent costs you nothing. They never become a complete, never enter your dataset, and never get paid. And because the check recognizes the **face**, not the device, they cannot try again from a new browser, a cleared cache, or a second phone. There is no side door to your survey.

## When they come back

Two different moments, two different answers, both working for you.

    <p className="mt-2 text-sm text-gray-500 dark:text-zinc-400">A dead end, on purpose. Their face already earned its one password, and that password is spent. Alchemer refuses it, and we refuse to mint another for the same face. One person, one response, permanently.</p>

Uniqueness still holds per study, and you are never charged twice for the same human.</p>
  </div>
</div>

**Why this matters to you:** repeat takers are locked out of each study forever, but your honest panelists are not punished for coming back. Your next wave fields faster, with less drop-off, at no extra verification cost. And if you want maximum strictness instead, a full re-scan on every study is a setting, and it is your call.
