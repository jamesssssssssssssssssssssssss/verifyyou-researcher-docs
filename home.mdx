<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Will it hurt my completion rate?">
    The check takes a few seconds, once. Verified respondents land in your survey with the password already filled in, so there is nothing to type and nothing to remember. Most of what a check turns away is the traffic you did not want anyway.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What does it cost?">
    About one to two cents a verification, charged once per human. On a \$10,000 sample of \$3 to \$7 completes, the whole check is a rounding error, and one caught repeat taker pays for hundreds of verifications.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I pay for people who get blocked?">
    No. A blocked respondent never receives a password, never reaches your survey, and never becomes a complete. Nothing to pay, nothing to clean out of your data.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can someone share the link or a password?">
    Sharing gets them nothing. Each password works exactly once, so a forwarded link carries a spent password, and the same face can never earn a second one for the same study. See [why it works](/how-it-works).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I ever see the respondent's face or personal information?">
    No. No photo is shared with you or with Alchemer, only a yes or no. Your survey receives a verdict and a password, never a face, a name, or contact details.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does this replace my attention checks?">
    No, and it is not trying to. It proves the person is real and unique, not that they answered carefully. Keep your speeder and attention checks; this closes the door they cannot close.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="When exactly does a password count as used?">
    A password is spent the moment it opens your survey's gate, not at completion. If someone starts your survey and abandons it, that password is gone, and because the check recognizes their face, they simply receive a fresh one on their next visit. Honest respondents are never locked out of a study they never finished.
  </Accordion>
